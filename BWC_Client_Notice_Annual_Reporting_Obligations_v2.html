Annual Reporting Obligations of BVI Business Companies

Every BVI business company has three recurring obligations each year: (1) payment of the annual government licence fee, (2) filing of an annual return with us as your registered agent, and (3) filing of an economic substance declaration. This note summarises what is due, when, and what happens if a deadline is missed.

1. Annual Government Licence Fee

The due date depends on the month in which your company was incorporated. Late payment attracts escalating penalties, and continued non-payment leads to the company being struck off the Register.

Incorporated Fee due by 10% penalty 50% penalty Struck off
January – June 31 May 1 June – 31 July 1 August – 31 October 1 November
July – December 30 November 1 December – 31 January 1 February – 30 April 1 May

If a company is struck off: restoration within six months costs US$500; after six months, US$1,500. A company struck off for more than seven years is dissolved by the Government of the British Virgin Islands.

2. Annual Return (Section 98A, BVI Business Companies Act)

Since 1 January 2023, every BVI company must file an annual return with its registered agent within nine months of its financial year end. Key points:

  • The return is a simple balance sheet and income statement. It does not need to be audited and is not made publicly available.
  • As registered agent, we must notify the Financial Services Commission if we have not received your return within 30 days of the due date.
  • Late filing attracts fines of up to US$5,000 in aggregate. Once the maximum fine has accrued, the Registrar may strike the company off the Register.
  • The filing fee is US$150 per company per year.
  • Companies listed on a recognised stock exchange are exempt.

3. Economic Substance Declaration

Every BVI company must file an annual economic substance declaration. The declaration is due within six months of the end of the company’s financial period. The financial period depends on when the company was incorporated:

  • Companies incorporated before January 2019: the default financial period runs from 30 June to 29 June each year, so the declaration is due by 31 December.
  • Companies incorporated from January 2019 onwards: the financial period starts on the date of incorporation and ends one year later; the declaration is due within six months of that end date.

Example: for a company incorporated on 1 May, the economic substance financial period is 1 May to 30 April, and the declaration is due by 31 October.

If you are unsure of your company’s financial period, please contact us and we will confirm it.
