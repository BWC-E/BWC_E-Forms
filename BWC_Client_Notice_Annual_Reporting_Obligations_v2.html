Annual Reporting Obligations of BVI Business Companies

Every BVI business company has three recurring obligations each year: (1) payment of the annual government licence fee, (2) filing of an annual return with us as your registered agent, and (3) filing of an economic substance declaration. This note summarises what is due, when, and what happens if a deadline is missed. For clarity, the fees quoted below are those payable to the BVI Government and the Financial Services Commission only, and do not include our own professional fees for preparing or filing on your behalf; a separate note of our fees is available on request.

1. Annual Government Licence Fee

The due date depends on the month in which your company was incorporated. Late payment attracts escalating penalties, and continued non-payment leads to the company being struck off the Register.

Incorporated Fee due by 10% penalty 50% penalty Struck off
January – June 31 May 1 June – 31 July 1 August – 31 October 1 November
July – December 30 November 1 December – 31 January 1 February – 30 April 1 May

If a company is struck off: restoration within six months of the strike-off date costs US$500; thereafter, US$1,500. Under the BVI Business Companies (Amendment) Act, 2022 (in force since 1 January 2023), a struck-off company is now dissolved automatically once the Registrar publishes the striking-off notice in the Gazette, and the window to apply to restore the company to the Register has been shortened from seven to five years from the date of dissolution — administratively via the Registrar, or by application to the Court in limited circumstances. After that period, restoration is no longer available.

2. Annual Return (Section 98A, BVI Business Companies Act)

Since 1 January 2023, every BVI company must file an annual return with its registered agent within nine months of its financial year end. Key points:

  • The return is a simple balance sheet and income statement. It does not need to be audited and is not made publicly available.
  • As registered agent, we must notify the Financial Services Commission if we have not received your return within 30 days of the due date.
  • Late filing attracts a penalty of US$300 for the first month (or part) overdue, then US$200 for each further month (or part), up to a maximum of US$5,000 in aggregate. Once the maximum penalty has accrued and the return remains unfiled, the Registrar may strike the company off the Register.
  • The filing fee is US$150 per company per year.
  • Three categories of company are exempt: (i) companies listed on a recognised stock exchange; (ii) companies regulated under BVI financial services legislation that already provide financial statements to the Commission; and (iii) companies that file an annual tax return with the BVI Inland Revenue Department accompanied by financial statements.

3. Economic Substance Declaration

Every BVI company must file an annual economic substance declaration. The declaration is due within six months of the end of the company’s financial period. The financial period depends on when the company was incorporated:

  • Companies incorporated before January 2019: the default financial period runs from 30 June to 29 June each year, so the declaration is due by 31 December.
  • Companies incorporated from January 2019 onwards: the financial period starts on the date of incorporation and ends one year later; the declaration is due within six months of that end date.

Example: for a company incorporated on 1 May, the economic substance financial period is 1 May to 30 April, and the declaration is due by 31 October.

If you are unsure of your company’s financial period, please contact us and we will confirm it.

Failure to report, or the provision of false information, is treated seriously under the Economic Substance Act and the Beneficial Ownership Secure Search System Act and can result in significant financial penalties and, in serious cases, striking off or imprisonment.
