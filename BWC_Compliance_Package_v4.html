3. Governance Commitment

As a micro company licensed as a registered agent and TCSP in the British Virgin Islands, Bethel Worldwide Corporation maintains a risk-based AML/CFT/CPF compliance framework that is proportionate to the nature, size and complexity of the business, in accordance with BVI FSC guidance and the Anti-Money Laundering Terrorist Financing Code of Practice (AMLTFCOP).

The framework is:

  • Practical and manageable for the Principal Director to implement, monitor and maintain;
  • Regularly reviewed and tested through annual self-assessment and periodic independent review;
  • Communicated to auditors, competent authorities and third parties as required.
4. Key Compliance Responsibilities of the Principal Director
  1. Policy — maintain a Compliance Manual aligned with AMLTFCOP, the AML Regulations, the Regulatory Code, the FSC Act and FSC Guidance; review at least annually or on material change.
  2. CDD & risk — perform risk-based CDD on all clients (simplified for low-risk; enhanced for higher-risk, including PEPs and high-risk jurisdictions); document each relationship.
  3. Beneficial ownership — maintain accurate, current BO information for all entities for which Bethel acts as registered agent; ensure corporate records are retrievable without undue delay.
  4. Sanctions — screen all clients and BOs against UN, UK OFSI and BVI designations at onboarding, at least annually, and on each new designation notice; freeze and report immediately on a positive match.
  5. SARs — identify, assess and escalate suspicious transactions/activities; file with the BVI FIA where the threshold is met; maintain an internal SAR log (Log 4).
  6. Ongoing monitoring — risk-based periodic review of active relationships (low-risk every 2–3 years; high-risk annually; trigger-based on material change).
  7. Internal control testing — annual sample review of client files (Log 5); document findings and remediation.
  8. Training — complete at least one substantive AML/CFT/CPF training event or guided review per calendar year (Log 6).
  9. Regulatory filings & inspections — file all annual returns on time; respond promptly to FSC requests; prepare inspection pack (Log 7) within FSC timeframes.
  10. Record-keeping — retain CDD, transaction and client-file records for at least 5 years post-relationship.

5. Customer Due Diligence (CDD) and Risk Assessment

When CDD is performed

  • At the commencement of a business relationship (e.g. a new client company incorporated);
  • For any one-off transaction;
  • Upon suspicion of ML/TF/PF;
  • Where a relationship presents higher-than-normal risk;
  • Where there is doubt about existing customer information.

CDD process (per Log 2 — CDD Checklist)

  1. Identify the applicant for business — entity type and documents (passport / national ID for individuals; Certificate of Incorporation and M&A for corporates; trust deed for trusts).
  2. Identify and verify beneficial owners — ≥25% holders, controllers, senior managing officials where ownership is dispersed.
  3. Understand purpose & nature — source of funds, source of wealth where appropriate, business activity.
  4. Risk-rate the relationship (Low / Moderate / High) using the matrix in Annex G.
  5. Decide — accept (with measures), seek further info, or decline.

Simplified vs Enhanced CDD

SDD may be applied to clearly low-risk relationships (e.g. regulated intermediaries in equivalent jurisdictions) with documented justification. EDD is required for PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, complex/opaque ownership and unusual transaction patterns; it includes senior-management approval, source-of-wealth verification and enhanced ongoing monitoring.

6. Beneficial Ownership

BWC maintains an internal BO register for every administered entity and ensures BVI BO-system entries are kept current. Changes in BO, controllers or structure are documented and updated promptly. Red flags include: bearer-share legacy; rapid changes in ownership without commercial rationale; nominee structures without verifiable rationale; reluctance to disclose UBO.

7. Sanctions Screening

Screening covers UN, UK OFSI and BVI designations. Performed at onboarding, at least annually, and immediately on a new designation notice. Positive matches trigger freezing, transaction restriction and reporting to the FSC, FIA and Governor's Office without delay (Log 3 — Sanctions Screening Log).

8. Ongoing Monitoring

Risk-based review schedule: low-risk every 2–3 years; high-risk annually; trigger-based on material change. Monitoring covers changes in client profile, BO, business activities and unusual corporate-activity patterns (e.g. multiple registered-office changes without operational reason). Documented in client file and Log 5.

9. Suspicious Activity

The PD (as MLRO) receives internal reports, assesses against AML/CFT/CPF indicators, and files a SAR/STR with the BVI FIA where the threshold is met. Confidentiality and the prohibition on tipping-off are observed. The internal SAR Log (Log 4) records all assessments — filed and not-filed.

10. Record-Keeping and Data Security

Records retained for at least 5 years post-relationship: CDD documents; BO verification; risk assessments; transaction documents; correspondence; SAR assessments; sanctions screening evidence; training records. Held electronically with access restricted to the PD; backed up; physical originals at the registered office or secure controlled location. Destruction at end-of-period is logged.

11. Third-Party Relationships and Reliance

Reliance on third-party CDD is permitted only where the third party is regulated for AML/CFT in an equivalent jurisdiction, has provided written confirmation, and CDD documentation can be obtained without delay on request. The relationship is reviewed annually.

BWC-SAR Not started

Confidential. Every internal suspicion is logged — whether or not it results in a filing with the BVI Financial Investigation Agency (FIA). Filed in a restricted location. Disclosure only to the FIA, FSC or law-enforcement on lawful request.

Tipping-off prohibition The MLRO and any other person aware of an internal SAR or external filing must not disclose its existence or content to the subject or any third party. Breach is a criminal offence under the Proceeds of Criminal Conduct Act.
